Feathered Flings

A Poem

By Sanaa Lott

Feather flings, in the sky they sing
A voice that attracts all with a grin
A pure white creature as happy as can be
Jumped as their ears laid upon that melody
One little rabbit
As small as a bean,
hopped out of their comfy den for all to see
Unfortunate the rabbit didn't suspect,
That the song would put him right under the Flings' trap
One dove at what seemed like the speed of light
Scooping at the little rabbit,
And vanished without a trace.
The mother screams for her child,
There was no use,
For the Feathered Flings will make sure,
Her baby will return with nothing more.
